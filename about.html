ABOUT WORKS BIO

Rebecca Acone is an emerging artist and a multidisciplinary designer whose main focus is on interactive tangible UI/UX and the intersection of humans and machines. Grounded in a careful practice of observation, she pays close attention to how people interact with systems to inform designs that are intuitive. Her work often explores these interactions, questioning how interfaces shape perception, behavior, and our understanding of reality. This practice plays a key role in her process and experimentations, allowing her to identify subtle gestures in everyday interactions. These observations are translated into interactive systems that respond to and reflect on human presence in a nuanced way. Driven by an interest in Human-Machine Interaction, she explores how design can facilitate meaningful and emotionally resonant connections between users and technology.

While her toolkit mainly includes experimentation in tangible UI/UX design, fabrication, and the construction of electronic circuits, she’s currently expanding her practice in creating walking sims in virtual environments, video editing archived and personal video content, and live visual performances. Her background in graphic design coupled with her current studies in Computation Arts and Philosophy at Concordia University deepen and support her explorations, and provide a solid foundation in the technical and creative aspects of digital art and technology. Through her ongoing practice, she aims to create interactive experiences that invite users to question the evolving relationship between themselves and reality.
