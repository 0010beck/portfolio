FEEL-EFFECT SYSTEMS (ONGOING)

feel-effect systems (w/ Kamyar Karimi) is an ongoing investigation of cybernetics and our relationship with the machine. In this new age of feedback, automation, and control, it is increasingly important to critique and to experiment with modes of power. The audio-visual performance set stages interaction loops between bodies, signals, and automated decision-making, drawing on observing systems and interaction loop theory. These loops are both the structure of the performance and its subject. They track how control is distributed, felt, resisted, and internalized, and how agency shifts when interfaces translate sensation into data and data into action. The work keeps the system exposed rather than smooth or transparent, so that limits, failures, and handoffs become part of what the audience can hear and see.
