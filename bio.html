ABOUT WORKS BIO / CV

Rebecca Acone is an emerging artist and designer (Montreal, QC) with a focus on tangible UI/UX. After recently finishing her undergrad at Concordia University, she’s actively pursuing interactive installation work and live visual performances. While her toolkit mainly includes experimentation in tangible UI/UX design, fabrication, and the construction of electronic circuits, she’s currently expanding her practice in creating walking sims in virtual environments, video editing archived and personal video content, and live visual performances. Her background in graphic design coupled with her current studies in Computation Arts and Philosophy at Concordia University deepen and support her explorations, and provide a solid foundation in the technical and creative aspects of digital art and technology. Through her ongoing practice, she aims to create interactive experiences that invite users to question the evolving relationship between themselves and reality.

She’s exhibited and performed in institutions and venues such as at World Creation Studio, Societe des arts technologiques (SAT), Concordia University’s Black Box and 4th Space, La Sotterenea, and NOMADlife.tv. She started exhibiting her work in 2023, and has since continued installation work and has further explored live performing visuals. She’s received recognition for her collaborative and interactive installation works at the D/CART end of year exhibition Nodes.Noeuds and at MUSICMOTION’s HACKLAB24, both held at the SAT. She has also been an active member of collectives, including the Computation Arts Student Society and Sandbox Collective, where she has collaborated with members on creative projects and curation, and contributed to community-driven events. She currently holds a BFA in Computation Arts Specialization with a Minor in Philosophy (Concordia University, Montreal, QC CAN).
